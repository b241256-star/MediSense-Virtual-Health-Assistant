⚠️ When to Seek Emergency Help Immediately

🫀 Heart & Chest

  • Severe chest pain or heavy pressure
  • Pain spreading to left arm or jaw
  • Sudden severe shortness of breath
  • Rapid or very irregular heartbeat
  • Fainting or near-fainting spell

🧠 Neurological Signs

  • Sudden severe "thunderclap" headache
  • Loss of consciousness or seizures
  • Sudden confusion or disorientation
  • Face drooping or arm weakness (Stroke!)
  • Slurred speech or vision changes

🩸 Bleeding & Trauma

  • Uncontrolled or heavy bleeding
  • Head injury with unconsciousness
  • Severe burns (2nd or 3rd degree)
  • Deep puncture wounds
  • Suspected broken bones

🫁 Breathing & Allergy

  • Severe difficulty breathing
  • Choking or airway obstruction
  • Anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction)
  • SpO2 oxygen level below 94%
  • High fever above 104°F (40°C)

🩹 Basic First Aid Steps

1

Stay Calm & Assess

Don't panic. Check the scene for safety. Assess the patient's consciousness and breathing.

2

Call for Help

Call 108 or 112 immediately. Describe the situation, location, and patient condition.

3

CPR if Needed

If not breathing: 30 chest compressions + 2 rescue breaths. Repeat until help arrives.

4

Control Bleeding

Apply firm pressure with clean cloth. Do NOT remove embedded objects. Elevate injured area.

5

Keep Patient Comfortable

Keep patient still, warm, and reassured. Don't give food or water. Monitor breathing.

6

Document & Inform

Note time of incident, medicines given, symptoms. Share with paramedics when they arrive.
